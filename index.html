Introduction

Mock trial is a competetive club which helps develop speaking and critical thinking skills, but it can be difficult to think about ways to argue effectively. Here are four tactics to help gain points when speaking during competition day.

Be Organized

Staying organized can help create an effective argument. It's necessary to have a logical, easy-to-follow structure to the argument. Making notes beforhand can help organize the evidence. Making an outline can help clarify the argument's flow and make sure the key ideas are mentioned.

Show Emotion

Emotion is an effective tool to persuade. Acting plays a big role in Mock Trial competitions. Passionate witnesses and lawyers will make an argument more convincing.

Have Confidence

Confidence can build trust with the judge and jury. Talk with clarity, make eye contact, and project a sense of expertise on the subject. If eye contact is uncomfortable, a helpful tip is to look in between the eyebrows. While confidence is important, also be aware of not comming off as arrogant because showing disrespect will lose the team points.

Be Adaptable

In Mock Trial, it is difficult to predict some of what the opposing side will say or what specific evidence they will present. It is important to be able to think quickly and maybe adjust some arguments if needed. Try to predict counterarguments and be ready to rebut them.
